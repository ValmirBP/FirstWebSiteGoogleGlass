Learn everything about Google Glass

by Valmir de Barros

What is it

Google Glass® is an accessory in the form of glasses that allows users to interact with various content in augmented reality. Also called Project Glass, The electronic device is capable of taking photos using voice commands, sending instant messages and carrying out video conferences. Its launch is scheduled for 2014, and its price should be US$1,500. Currently Google Glass is in the testing phase and already has a video completely recorded with the device. In addition, the search company registered new anti-theft and screen unlock patents for the accessory.

Release date

There is no specific and official date for the device to be launched yet. It may be available in demonstrations from 2013 onwards, but its launch in stores will be scheduled for at least 2014.

How it works

According to sources close to Google, the glasses will feature a small LCD or AMOLED screen on the top and in front of the user's eyes. Using a camera and GPS, you can locate yourself, as well as select options with the movement of the head

What you can do with Google Glasses

Google's promotional video shows that you can transform yourself into a kind of “super-human”, as the device can indicate how many meters you are from your destination, whether the subway is open or closed, show the weather, schedule and even allow you to schedule meetings with just voice commands.

What's New in Glass

Google has finally revealed the complete specifications of Google Glass, and with it a surprise that has never been seen before on the market: the search giant will use an audio system based on conduction transduction. Through the temples of the glasses, sound will be transmitted to the user's ear through microvibrations in certain bones in their head, without using any type of speaker.

In addition to the audio surprise, the screen mounted in front of the user's eye also caught attention. There will be 640 x 360 pixels of resolution which, in proportion, would be equivalent to a 25-inch high-definition monitor placed 2.5 meters away from the viewer.
